Your Paid Time Off (PTO)
PTO is your time for vacation, personal days, and any planned time away. It is separate from your Sick Leave bank and follows its own rules.
PTO and Sick Leave are tracked in two separate banks in ADP. Your full PTO balance is visible in ADP Self Service. Submit an HR Request Form with any questions about your balance.
How PTO Works
Granted on January 1

Your full annual PTO balance is available on January 1 each year. If you were hired mid-year, your first grant was prorated to your hire date. You will receive your first full annual allotment on your next January 1.

90-Day Waiting Period

New employees must complete 90 days of employment before using PTO. Your balance may already be visible in ADP, but it is not accessible until the 90-day mark. Sick leave has no waiting period.

Minimum 4-Hour Increments

PTO must be taken in increments of at least 4 hours. You cannot request less than a half-day of PTO at one time. Sick leave is more flexible and can be taken in 1-hour increments.

Carryover & Year-End

Up to 40 hours of unused PTO carries over into the next calendar year. Any amount above 40 hours is forfeited at year-end, where permitted by law. California employees: PTO is a vested wage and cannot be forfeited under state law.

Payout When You Leave

When your employment ends, you will receive a payout for accrued PTO. The payout is prorated to your separation date — not the full annual grant. Massachusetts and California employees: all accrued PTO must be paid out at separation.

California & Massachusetts

If you work in CA or MA, PTO accrues each pay period (weekly pay periods) rather than being front-loaded on January 1. Your state treats PTO as earned wages. Your balance appears on your pay statement and in ADP each pay period. Accrual cap: 1.5× your annual tier allotment.

Your PTO Allotment
Your PTO allotment is based on your job classification at Furnished Quarters. HR provides your individual PTO policy document at hire and whenever your classification changes. Your specific allotment details — including your starting balance and maximum — are in that document.
Your Balance Grows Each Year

Your PTO increases by 1 day (8 hours) for each completed year of service, up to the maximum outlined in your individual policy. Once the maximum is reached, your annual allotment stays the same every year.

Carryover Cap — All Employees

Up to 40 hours (5 days) of unused PTO carries into the next calendar year. Any balance above 40 hours is forfeited at year end. If your balance is running high, plan to use your time before December 31.

CA & MA Employees

If you work in California or Massachusetts, your PTO accrues per pay period rather than as a front-loaded annual grant. State law requires this accrual method. Contact HR with questions about your specific accrual schedule or balance.

Find Your Details in ADP

Your current PTO balance and policy tier are always visible in ADP WorkforceNow under Myself > Time Off. If you need a copy of your individual policy document or have questions about your tier, contact HR.

Your Sick Leave
Sick leave is a separate bank from PTO. It accrues continuously from your first day and covers illness, medical needs, and qualifying family care.
Key rule: Sick leave and PTO are completely separate. Using sick leave does not reduce your PTO, and using PTO does not reduce your sick leave. You can see both balances in ADP Self Service.

Minimum Increment

Sick leave can be used in increments as small as 1 hour. You do not need to take a full half-day or full day. This is more flexible than PTO, which requires a minimum of 4 hours.

Your Balance in ADP

Your sick leave balance appears in ADP Self Service alongside your PTO balance. If you work in a state that requires it, your sick leave balance will also appear on your pay statement each pay period.

What Sick Leave Can Be Used For
Your Own Health Illness, injury, medical appointments, and preventive care visits.
Family Care Caring for a family member with an illness, injury, or medical need, including preventive care visits.
Mental Health Mental health conditions are explicitly covered. Treat mental health needs the same as physical health needs.
Safe Time Situations involving domestic violence, sexual assault, stalking, or similar conduct affecting you or a family member.
Public Health Workplace closures or school/childcare closures due to a public health emergency.
Scheduled Medical Appointments Pre-planned appointments like physical therapy, chemotherapy, specialist visits, or annual physicals are permitted sick leave uses.
Documentation: You are not required to provide a diagnosis or detailed medical information. A doctor's note may only be requested after three or more consecutive workdays of absence, and only in coordination with HR policy and applicable law.
When Sick Leave Cannot Be Used
Sick leave is not additional vacation time. It may not be used to extend a vacation or holiday weekend, for personal plans or leisure, or to use up your balance before year end. If you want time off for personal reasons, submit a PTO request in ADP.

How Sick Leave and PTO Work Together
When you have a qualifying absence, sick leave and PTO follow a specific order. Understanding this helps you know what to expect and when to take action.
Core rule: Sick leave is applied first to any qualifying absence. You may use PTO for wage replacement only after sick leave is exhausted — and only if you request it. Floating holidays and the Floating Cultural Day may not be used in lieu of sick time, and may not be used for wage replacement when sick leave is exhausted.
Standard Process — All Employees
Step 1
Sick Leave Applied First
Available sick leave is applied to qualifying absences automatically. This is the company default. You do not need to request this step.
Step 2
Sick Balance Exhausted
Once your sick leave runs out, the absence continues. You are not automatically paid during this time — your absence continues without wage replacement unless you take the next step.
Step 3 — Your Option
You May Request PTO
After sick leave is exhausted, you may request to use available PTO for wage replacement. PTO is never applied automatically — the request must come from you. To make this request, log in to ADP Self Service and submit your time off using the reason code PTO — Call Out. You do not need to contact HR.

NYC Employees — Additional Layer: Protected Time Off

If you work in New York City, there is an additional layer of protection beyond the standard process. NYC employees are covered by two sick leave laws (ESSTA and NYPSSL) plus NYC Protected Time Off.

NYC Step 1
Paid Sick Leave Applied First
NYC employees have up to 56 hours (7 days) of paid sick/safe leave annually under ESSTA and NYPSSL, applied first to qualifying absences.
NYC Step 2
Protected Time Off Covers the Absence
After paid sick leave is exhausted, NYC Protected Time Off (32 hours) covers the absence. This leave is job-protected but unpaid. Your manager may not require you to use PTO during this period.
NYC Step 3 — Your Option
You May Elect PTO for Pay
You may choose to use available PTO for wage replacement. This must come from you. The absence is still counted against your NYC Protected Time Off bank, and that balance is reduced based on time away. Using PTO does not preserve or extend the 32-hour balance. Job protection remains in place regardless.
How to Request Time Off in ADP
All time off — both PTO and sick leave — is submitted through ADP Self Service. The process is slightly different depending on which bank you are using.

Requesting PTO

  • Log into ADP Self Service and submit a PTO request
  • Submit requests in minimum 4-hour increments
  • Give at least 30 days notice for foreseeable time off
  • Await your manager's approval before confirming plans
  • Check your balance in ADP Self Service at any time
  • PTO and Sick Leave balances appear side by side in ADP

Reporting Sick Leave

  • Use ADP Self Service to report sick time when you are out
  • Sick leave can be taken in increments as small as 1 hour
  • No waiting period — use sick leave as soon as it accrues
  • You do not need to provide a diagnosis or doctor's note
  • Pre-planned medical appointments should be submitted in advance
  • Contact HR with any questions about your balance or state rights
You will see two separate balances in ADP Self Service — one for PTO and one for Sick Leave. If you work in a state that requires it, your sick leave balance will also appear on your pay statement each pay period.
Requesting Wage Replacement After Sick Leave is Exhausted
If you have exhausted your sick leave and want to use PTO for wage replacement, log in to ADP Self Service and submit your time off using the reason code PTO — Call Out. PTO is never applied automatically after sick leave runs out — the request must come from you. You do not need to contact HR.

Common Questions
Answers to the questions employees ask most often about PTO and Sick Leave.
PTO Questions
  • When can I start using PTO? ▶
    New employees must wait 90 days before using PTO. Your balance may already be visible in ADP, but it is not accessible until the 90-day mark. Sick leave is different — there is no waiting period, and you can use sick leave as soon as it accrues from day one of employment.
  • What happens to unused PTO at year-end? ▶
    Up to 40 hours of unused PTO carries over into the new year. Any amount above 40 hours is forfeited at year-end, where permitted by law. California employees: PTO is a vested wage and cannot be forfeited under state law. Massachusetts employees: accrued PTO must be paid out at separation per the MA Wage Act.
  • Do I get my PTO paid out when I leave the company? ▶
    Yes. When your employment ends, you will receive a payout for accrued PTO. The payout is prorated to your actual separation date — it is not the full annual grant. Massachusetts and California employees are entitled to full payout of all accrued PTO under state law. Contact HR with any questions about your payout entitlement.
  • I work in California or Massachusetts — how is my PTO different? ▶
    If you work in CA or MA, your PTO accrues each pay period (weekly pay periods) rather than being granted in full on January 1. State law treats PTO as earned wages, which means it is tracked and cannot be forfeited (CA) or must be paid out at separation (MA). Your balance appears on your pay statement each pay period and in ADP. Your accrual cap is 1.5× your annual tier allotment. Contact HR with any questions about your accrual schedule.
Sick Leave Questions
  • What happens if my sick leave runs out? ▶
    Once your sick leave is exhausted, your absence continues but you are not automatically paid. You may request to use available PTO for wage replacement — this must come from you, it is never applied automatically. To make this request, log in to ADP Self Service and submit your time off using the reason code PTO — Call Out. You do not need to contact HR. If you work in New York City, there is an additional step: NYC Protected Time Off (a 32-hour flat grant) may cover your absence as job-protected but unpaid leave before you elect to use PTO.
  • Can I schedule sick leave in advance for a medical appointment? ▶
    Yes. Sick leave can be scheduled in advance when the purpose is a protected sick leave reason. This includes doctor appointments, specialist visits, physical therapy, chemotherapy, mental health care, preventive care, and caring for a family member. You do not need to provide a diagnosis. Documentation may only be requested for absences of three or more consecutive workdays, consistent with applicable law.
  • Do I have to tell my manager why I’m using sick leave, or provide my diagnosis? ▶
    No. You are not required to disclose your diagnosis, the nature of your illness, or the medical details of your condition. You only need to confirm that the absence is for a covered reason under sick leave policy — such as your own illness, a medical appointment, or caring for a family member. A doctor’s note or medical documentation may only be requested after three or more consecutive workdays of absence, and even then, only in coordination with HR policy and applicable state law. If your manager asks for your diagnosis or medical details in connection with a routine sick leave request, please contact HR.
  • Can I use a floating holiday or the Floating Cultural Day when my sick leave runs out or I don’t have enough sick time? ▶
    No. Floating holidays and the Floating Cultural Day may not be used for wage replacement when your sick leave is exhausted or when your sick leave balance is insufficient to cover an absence. These benefits are designed for scheduled personal or cultural observances — they are not a substitute for sick leave or PTO in a qualified sick absence scenario. If your sick leave runs out or there is not enough to cover your absence, you may request PTO for wage replacement by logging into ADP Self Service and submitting your time off using the reason code PTO — Call Out.
Paid Holiday Questions
  • How many paid holidays does the company observe, and where can I find the dates? ▶
    Furnished Quarters observes seven paid holidays each calendar year: New Year’s Day, Memorial Day, Independence Day, Labor Day, Thanksgiving Day, the Day After Thanksgiving, and Christmas Day. The Company publishes an annual holiday calendar each year identifying the official observance dates. That calendar is the governing document for each year’s holiday dates. When a holiday falls on a Saturday, it is generally observed on the preceding Friday; when it falls on a Sunday, it is generally observed on the following Monday.
  • I have approved PTO scheduled during a company holiday — will the holiday count against my PTO? ▶
    No. If a recognized holiday falls during a period when you have approved PTO, the day is treated as a paid holiday and is not deducted from your PTO balance. Your PTO remains intact as though the holiday had not occurred. If your ADP balance does not reflect this correctly, submit an HR Request Form and HR will correct it.
  • I was asked to work on a recognized holiday — how does that affect my pay? ▶
    Non-exempt employees who work on a recognized holiday receive one and one-half times their regular rate of pay for all hours actually worked that day. In addition to the premium pay, eligible employees who work on a holiday receive an alternate paid day off in lieu of the holiday. That alternate day must generally be scheduled within 14 calendar days of the observed holiday and must be coordinated with your manager. If you have questions about how your holiday pay was processed, contact HR.
  • What is the Floating Cultural Day and how do I use it? ▶
    The Floating Cultural Day is a separate 8-hour benefit provided annually to eligible full-time employees. It is granted on January 1 each year (or upon completing your 90-day introductory period for new hires). You can use it for a birthday, religious or cultural holiday, or any other occasion that is personally meaningful to you. It can be taken as a full day or in 4-hour increments. Submit your request through ADP in advance and obtain your manager’s approval. The day must be used within the calendar year, does not carry over, and is not paid out at termination.
  • What is the difference between the Floating Cultural Day and floating holidays? ▶
    They are two separate benefits. Floating holidays are granted based on your hire date — 2 or 3 upon hire (depending on whether you started before or after July 1), and 4 on each January 1 thereafter. The Floating Cultural Day is an additional 8-hour benefit provided separately, also on January 1 each year. Both must be used within the calendar year, do not carry over, and are not paid out at termination. You can see both balances in ADP Self Service or contact HR with any questions.
General Questions
  • Where do I see my PTO and Sick Leave balances? ▶
    Log into ADP Self Service. You will see two separate balances in ADP Self Service — one for PTO and one for Sick Leave. If you work in a state that requires sick leave to appear on your pay stub, it will also be displayed there each pay period.
  • Does using PTO count against my sick leave? ▶
    No. PTO and Sick Leave are completely independent banks. Using PTO does not reduce your Sick Leave balance, and using Sick Leave does not reduce your PTO balance. They are tracked separately in ADP.
  • Who do I contact if I have questions? ▶
    Submit an HR Request using the HR Request Form with any questions about your balances, your state rights, how to submit time off in ADP, or anything else about PTO and Sick Leave.

Eligibility & Holiday Pay
Who Is Eligible

Regular full-time employees are eligible for paid holiday benefits.

Regular part-time employees receive holiday pay only if the holiday falls on a day they are regularly scheduled to work.

Employees must be actively employed at the time of the holiday to receive holiday pay, unless otherwise required by applicable law.

How Holiday Pay Works

Holiday pay is based on the number of hours you were scheduled to work on the observed holiday, up to a maximum of 8 hours.

Holiday pay for time not worked does not count as hours worked for overtime calculation purposes, unless required by applicable law.

Holiday During PTO

If a recognized holiday falls during an approved PTO period, the day is treated as a holiday and is not deducted from your PTO balance.

If a holiday falls during an unpaid leave of absence, holiday pay is provided only where required by applicable law.

If a holiday falls during a legally protected leave, eligibility is administered in accordance with applicable law.

Working on a Holiday

Some departments may be required to maintain staffing on recognized holidays based on business needs.

Non-exempt employees who work on a holiday receive 1.5x their regular rate for all hours actually worked that day.

In addition to premium pay, eligible employees who work on a holiday receive an alternate paid day off, which must generally be scheduled within 14 calendar days of the observed holiday.

Floating Holidays
Full-time eligible employees receive up to four floating holidays per calendar year. Floating holidays may be used to observe religious or cultural holidays, birthdays, or other days of personal significance, subject to manager approval and business needs.

Bereavement Leave
Bereavement leave is separate from your PTO and Sick Leave banks. Taking bereavement leave does not reduce either of those balances.
How it works: When you experience a loss, please notify your manager and HR as soon as you are able. Bereavement leave is granted on top of your PTO and sick leave — you are not required to use either of those banks first. If you need more time beyond what the policy provides, you may separately request PTO.
