MAIN COMPANY — PTO Policies (All States Except CA & MA)

Grant-based (front-loaded). Awarded annually on January 1. New hires receive a prorated allotment from their hire date. For administrative purposes, PTO is treated as earned on a pro-rata basis throughout the year. Carryover capped at 40 hours; excess forfeited where law permits. 90-day waiting period to use. Min increment: 4 hours. Payout on separation: Company pays out PTO accrued through the separation date. No pay statement requirement.

MASSACHUSETTS — PTO Policies (Accrual-Based; MA Wage Act Governs)

Accrues incrementally each payroll period starting from date of hire. Active work only; no accrual during unpaid LOA. Once accrued, PTO is considered earned wages under MA law. Accrual cap = 1.5× annual amount. Payout required on separation. ADP Self-Service used for requests (30-day advance notice required). Min increment: 4 hours. 90-day waiting period.

CALIFORNIA — PTO Policies (Accrual; Vested Wage; No Use-It-or-Lose-It)

Accrues per payroll period from date of hire. PTO is a vested wage under CA law — forfeiture is prohibited. Accrual cap = 1.5× annual amount (accrual pauses at cap; resumes when balance drops below cap). Unused PTO carries over year to year with no year-end reduction. Full payout required on separation at final rate of pay. CA law requires PTO balance on each itemized wage statement or separate writing on each pay date. Min increment: 4 hours. 90-day waiting period.

MAIN COMPANY — General Sick Leave Policy

Separate bank from PTO. State and local addenda control where they provide greater rights. Accrual begins day 1 of employment. Exempt employees presumed 40 hrs/wk for accrual. Min increment: 1 hour. Employees may use PTO after sick leave is exhausted.

👤 Questions from Employees
Where do I see my PTO and sick leave balances? ▶
Both balances are visible in ADP Self Service. Employees see two separate balances side by side — one for PTO and one for Sick Leave. Employees in states that require it will also see their sick leave balance on their pay statement each pay period. If an employee cannot find their balances, direct them to ADP Self Service and confirm their profile is updated. Contact HR if the balance appears incorrect.
When can I start using PTO? (new hire question) ▶
New employees must complete 90 days of employment before using PTO. The balance may already be visible in ADP during this period but cannot be used until the 90-day mark. Sick leave has no waiting period — it accrues from day one and can be used as soon as it accrues. This is a common question from new hires who see a PTO balance but cannot yet access it.
What happens to unused PTO at year-end? ▶
Up to 40 hours of unused PTO carries into the next calendar year. Any amount above 40 hours is forfeited at year-end where permitted by law. California exception: PTO is a vested wage and cannot be forfeited under CA state law — do not advise CA employees that PTO will be forfeited. Massachusetts exception: Accrued PTO is treated as a vested wage under the MA Wage Act and must be paid out at separation.
What if my sick leave runs out during an absence? ▶
Once sick leave is exhausted, the absence continues without automatic pay. The employee is not automatically moved to PTO. The employee may request to use available PTO for wage replacement — this must come from the employee. The employee can make this request by logging into ADP Self Service and submitting their time off using the reason code PTO — Call Out. They do not need to contact HR to initiate this request. NYC employees have an additional step: NYC Protected Time Off (32 hrs, flat grant) covers the absence as job-protected but unpaid leave before the employee elects to use PTO. The Protected Time Off bank is reduced based on time away, even when PTO is used for pay. Job protection remains regardless.
Can I schedule sick leave in advance for a medical appointment? ▶
Yes — sick leave can be scheduled in advance for any protected sick leave reason. Protected reasons include: scheduled doctor, dentist, or specialist appointments; ongoing treatment (physical therapy, chemotherapy); mental health care; preventive care; and caring for a family member. The employee does not need to provide a diagnosis. Sick leave cannot be used to extend a vacation, for personal plans, or to use up the bank before year-end. Those are PTO purposes.
Does my state give me more sick leave than the main policy? ▶
Possibly. State and local laws provide additional protections in: Arizona, California, Connecticut, Illinois, Massachusetts, Michigan, Minnesota (including Minneapolis), New Jersey, New York State, and New York City. ADP is configured to apply the correct rules automatically based on the employee's work location. Employees do not need to manage this themselves. If an employee asks about their specific state entitlement, direct them to HR rather than guessing. See the Sick Leave — States tab for full jurisdiction details.
Will I get paid out for sick leave when I leave the company? ▶
No — sick leave is not paid out at separation under the main company policy. Sick leave is a benefit that expires when employment ends. PTO payout rules: Main Company employees receive a prorated PTO payout through their separation date (per offer letter terms). Massachusetts and California employees receive full payout of all accrued PTO at separation under state law.
I work in California / Massachusetts — how is my PTO different? ▶
CA and MA employees accrue PTO per weekly pay period rather than receiving a front-loaded grant on January 1. State law treats PTO as earned wages. Key differences:
  • CA: PTO cannot be forfeited. All accrued PTO must be paid out at separation. Accrual cap is 1.5× annual tier amount.
  • MA: Accrued PTO is a vested wage under the MA Wage Act. All accrued PTO must be paid out at separation. Both PTO and sick leave balances appear on pay statements.
Employees in these states see their balances in ADP and on pay stubs each pay period. HR manages these accrual schedules — employees do not need to calculate manually.
📍 Questions from Managers
Can I deny a sick leave request? ▶
Generally, no — managers should not deny sick leave for qualifying reasons. If an employee is absent for illness, injury, medical appointment, family care, safe time, or public health reasons, the absence is protected under company policy and, in many states, under law. Managers must contact HR before denying any sick leave request, especially for employees in regulated states (AZ, CA, CT, IL, MA, MI, MN, NJ, NY/NYC). Denial without HR review creates legal risk. Do not ask probing questions, request a diagnosis, or require the employee to find their own coverage as a condition of sick leave approval.
What documentation can I ask for? ▶
Managers may not require documentation for a single-day absence. Documentation (such as a return-to-work note) may only be requested after three or more consecutive workdays of absence, and only in coordination with HR. Managers may never ask for a diagnosis, the nature of a medical condition, or detailed medical information. Requesting this information can violate the ADA, FMLA, and various state laws. Always contact HR before requesting any documentation from an employee.
How do I approve time off in ADP? ▶
All time-off requests — both PTO and sick leave — are submitted and approved through ADP WorkforceNow. Managers should:
  • Review pending requests in ADP WorkforceNow promptly (within 2 business days is best practice)
  • Approve or decline based on staffing and business needs for PTO; approve sick leave for qualifying reasons
  • Ensure all approved or declined decisions are recorded in ADP — email or verbal approvals alone are not sufficient
  • Contact HR if an absence is complex, extended, or may qualify for FMLA or another leave program
An employee wants to use PTO instead of sick leave — can I allow it? ▶
No. Company policy and state law in most jurisdictions require sick leave to be applied first to qualifying absences. Managers may not allow an employee to use PTO in place of sick leave while sick leave is still available. Doing so would create legal liability in regulated states and policy violations company-wide. The correct order is:
  1. Sick leave is applied first (automatic)
  2. If sick is exhausted, the absence continues
  3. Employee may then request PTO for wage replacement (employee-initiated only)
If an employee insists, direct the question to HR.
Can I require employees to find their own coverage before calling out sick? ▶
No. Managers may not require an employee to find or arrange their own coverage as a condition of using sick leave. This is explicitly prohibited under most state sick leave laws and company policy. Coverage is a management responsibility. Managers should have a coverage plan for their team that does not depend on sick employees remaining at work or coordinating replacements before leaving.
I think an employee is misusing sick leave. What do I do? ▶
Contact HR before taking any action. Managers should not independently address suspected sick leave misuse. Document the patterns you have observed (specific dates, frequency, surrounding circumstances) and bring them to HR. HR will assess whether the pattern meets the threshold to request documentation or take any corrective action, consistent with applicable law. Retaliation against an employee for using protected sick leave — even suspected misuse — creates significant legal exposure. Never discipline, penalize, or threaten an employee for sick leave use without HR guidance.
An employee's absence might qualify for FMLA. What do I do? ▶
Contact HR immediately. Managers do not determine FMLA eligibility — HR does. If an employee's absence is for a serious health condition (their own or a family member's), has lasted more than three consecutive days, or is part of a chronic condition pattern, it may trigger FMLA notice obligations. Managers must not ignore potential FMLA triggers. Even if the employee does not mention FMLA by name, HR must be notified if the circumstances could qualify. FMLA leave runs concurrently with sick leave — HR will manage the coordination in ADP.
An employee called out sick on a holiday or scheduled day off. Does sick leave apply? ▶
Generally, sick leave is not charged for a day the employee was not otherwise scheduled to work (including company holidays, scheduled days off, or days outside their regular schedule). If an employee calls out sick on a day they were scheduled and it happens to be adjacent to a holiday, sick leave applies to the scheduled workdays only. The holiday itself is covered as a company holiday — not charged to sick leave. Contact HR if the situation involves an extended absence that spans multiple days including holidays.
An employee in NYC is out. How does sick leave, Protected Time Off, and PTO work together? ▶
NYC employees have four potential layers of leave. The correct process is:
  • Step 1: ESSTA and/or NYPSSL paid sick leave (up to 56 hrs/yr combined) is applied first to qualifying absences
  • Step 2: If sick leave is exhausted, NYC Protected Time Off (32 hrs, flat annual grant) covers the absence — job-protected but unpaid. Managers may NOT require the employee to use PTO during this step
  • Step 3: The employee may elect to use PTO for wage replacement. This request must come from the employee. The Protected Time Off bank is reduced based on time away, even when PTO is used for pay. Job protection remains in place regardless
Direct all NYC extended absences to HR for ADP processing. Do not attempt to manage the coding sequence independently.
Can an employee use floating holidays instead of sick leave when sick leave is exhausted or insufficient? ▶
No. Floating holidays and the Floating Cultural Day may not be used in lieu of sick time, and may not be used for wage replacement when sick leave is exhausted or when the sick leave balance is insufficient to cover an absence. This applies whether the sick bank is completely empty or simply does not have enough hours to cover the full absence. After sick leave runs out or is insufficient, the employee may only request PTO for wage replacement using the reason code PTO — Call Out in ADP Self Service. Floating holidays and the Floating Cultural Day serve a different purpose and are not substitutes for sick leave or PTO in a qualified sick absence scenario. If a manager or employee requests to use floating holidays or the Floating Cultural Day this way, decline and direct the question to HR.
🏢 Paid Holidays & Floating Days — HR Questions
How are the seven recognized paid holidays set up in ADP? ▶
Paid holidays are coded as holiday pay in ADP and are separate from the PTO and Sick Leave accrual banks. Eligible employees receive holiday pay automatically based on their scheduled hours for the observed holiday date (up to 8 hours). Non-exempt employees who work on a recognized holiday must be coded for both holiday premium pay (1.5x) and an alternate paid day off. The alternate day off must be scheduled within 14 calendar days of the observed holiday — coordinate with the employee's manager and process in ADP. Consult ADP WorkforceNow documentation or HR leadership for the specific pay code configuration for your payroll setup.
What is the ADP bank name for the Floating Cultural Day, and how is it granted? ▶
The Floating Cultural Day is tracked in ADP under the bank name Floating Cultural Day. On January 1 each year, eligible full-time regular employees who have completed their 90-Day Introductory Period receive an 8-hour grant. For new hires who complete their 90-day period mid-year, the 8 hours should be loaded at that time if the employee remains active in the same calendar year. Unused hours do not carry over and should be zeroed out at year-end. The bank is separate from floating holidays — do not combine them in ADP.
An employee's PTO was reduced during a week that included a recognized holiday. How do I fix it? ▶
Per policy, if a recognized holiday falls during an approved PTO period, the holiday must be treated as a paid holiday and the PTO balance should not be reduced for that day. If ADP incorrectly deducted PTO for the holiday date, correct it manually in the employee's time-off record. Add the hours back to the PTO bank and code the day as holiday pay instead. Notify the employee once corrected so they are aware their balance has been restored.
How do we handle holiday eligibility for part-time employees? ▶
Regular part-time employees are eligible for holiday pay only if the holiday falls on a day they are regularly scheduled to work. Before approving or processing holiday pay for a part-time employee, verify their standard schedule. If the holiday falls on a day they are not scheduled, they do not receive holiday pay for that day. If the holiday falls on a day they are scheduled, pay them for their scheduled hours up to the 8-hour maximum. Document the schedule review in case of questions.
A holiday falls during an employee's approved leave of absence. Do they receive holiday pay? ▶
It depends on the type of leave. If the leave is an approved PTO period: yes, the day is treated as a paid holiday and is not charged to PTO. If the leave is an unpaid leave of absence: holiday pay is provided only where required by applicable law. If the leave is a legally protected leave (such as FMLA or a state-protected leave): holiday eligibility is administered in accordance with applicable law. Always consult HR leadership or employment counsel before making eligibility determinations for employees on protected leaves.
What is the difference between floating holidays and the Floating Cultural Day in ADP? ▶
These are two distinct benefits that must be tracked in separate ADP banks. Floating Holidays: Full-time employees receive 3 hours upon hire (if hired before July 1) or 2 hours (if hired on/after July 1), then 4 floating holidays on each January 1 thereafter. ADP bank name: Floating Holiday (or as configured). Floating Cultural Day: Full-time employees receive 8 hours on January 1 each year (or upon completing the 90-day period for new hires), in a separate bank named Floating Cultural Day. Never combine these into the same ADP bank. Both expire December 31 and are not paid out at termination. Track them independently.
