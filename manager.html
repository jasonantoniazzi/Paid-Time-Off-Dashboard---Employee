What Managers Need to Know About PTO Tiers
Each employee is assigned to one of four PTO tiers based on their job classification. HR manages tier assignment and provides each employee with their individual policy document. Your role is to work with what ADP shows you — not to share specific tier amounts across the team.
Tier amounts are confidential to each employee. Do not share one employee's tier allocation with another. If an employee has questions about their specific allotment, direct them to HR. You can see balances and usage in ADP — that is all you need to manage approvals effectively.
Tier Assignment

Each employee is assigned to a tier (B, C, D, or E) by HR based on their job classification. The assignment is updated whenever a reclassification occurs. You can see each employee's tier clearly labeled in their ADP profile.

Check Balances in ADP

Log into ADP WorkforceNow and navigate to My Team → Time Off Balances. From there you can view each team member's current PTO tier, balance, and usage history for the year.

PTO Grows Each Year

Every employee's PTO increases by 1 day (8 hours) for each completed year of service, up to the maximum for their individual tier. The carryover cap — 40 hours — applies uniformly to all tiers.

CA & MA Employees

Employees in California or Massachusetts accrue PTO per pay period rather than receiving a front-loaded annual grant. State law requires this method. A higher balance cap also applies. ADP handles this automatically — no action needed from you.

The method of PTO delivery and the carry-over rules differ based on where an employee works. ADP handles the correct setup automatically — your job is to approve or deny requests through ADP, not by email or verbal agreement alone.

Key PTO Rules for Managers
Front-Loaded on January 1

Main employees receive their full annual allotment on January 1. Employees hired mid-year receive a prorated amount based on their hire date. ADP calculates the proration automatically.

40-Hour Carryover Cap (Main)

Main employees may carry up to 40 hours (5 days) of unused PTO into the next calendar year. Any balance above 40 hours is forfeited at year end. Encourage your team to plan ahead if their balance is running high. Do not deny reasonable vacation requests.

Tenure Steps Up Each Year

Every completed year of service adds 8 hours to the annual allotment, up to the tier maximum. ADP updates balances automatically on each employee's anniversary date.

Paid Out at Separation

Accrued PTO is paid out upon any separation. For Main employees, this is the amount granted on Jan 1 minus hours used through the exit date. Contact HR immediately when an employee separation is confirmed.

90-Day Waiting Period

Newly hired employees may not use PTO during their first 90 days of employment. PTO may appear visible in ADP during this period, but it cannot be accessed until the 90-day mark. Sick leave can be used as soon as it accrues.

All Requests Through ADP

All PTO requests must be submitted by the employee through ADP WorkforceNow. You approve or deny through ADP — not by email, text, or verbal agreement alone. The ADP record is the official record.

How Sick Leave and PTO Work Together
Sick leave and PTO are separate banks. Company policy establishes the order in which they are applied. Understanding this process is essential for managing absences correctly — especially for employees in New York City.
Core rule: Sick leave is applied first to any qualifying absence. Employees may request to use PTO for wage replacement only after their sick leave balance is exhausted. Floating holidays and the Floating Cultural Day may not be used in lieu of sick time, and may not be used for wage replacement when sick leave is exhausted.
Standard Process — All Employees
Step 1
Sick Leave Applied First
When an employee is absent for a qualifying reason, available sick leave is applied first. This is the company default and aligns with most state requirements. No action needed from you.
Step 2
Sick Balance Exhausted
Once sick leave is fully used, any additional qualifying absence continues. The employee is not automatically paid unless PTO is applied. The absence continues without wage replacement.
Step 3 — Employee Option
Employee May Request PTO
After sick leave is exhausted, an employee may request to use available PTO for wage replacement. This request must come from the employee. Do not apply PTO on their behalf without their request. Direct the employee to log in to ADP Self Service and submit their time off using the reason code PTO — Call Out.
Manager rule: Never apply PTO to an employee's absence automatically. Never pressure or require an employee to use PTO after sick leave is exhausted. The election must come from the employee.

NYC Employees — Additional Layer: Protected Time Off

New York City employees have a third leave type in addition to ESSTA sick leave and PTO: NYC Protected Time Off — a 32-hour annual flat grant. This leave is unpaid by design and is job-protected.

NYC Step 1
Paid Sick Applied First
For any qualifying absence, available NYC ESSTA and/or NY State NYPSSL paid sick leave is applied first.
NYC Step 2
Protected Time Off Covers the Absence
If sick leave is exhausted, NYC Protected Time Off covers the absence. The employee's job is protected, but the leave is unpaid. You may not require the employee to use PTO during this period.
NYC Step 3 — Employee Option
Employee May Elect PTO for Wage Replacement
The employee may choose to use available PTO to receive pay. This election must come from the employee. The absence is still counted against the NYC Protected Time Off bank, and that balance is reduced based on time away. Using PTO does not preserve or extend the 32-hour balance. Job protection remains in place regardless. Direct the employee to HR to process the PTO election correctly in ADP.

Key rule: Never deny sick leave to an employee in a protected state without checking with HR first. When in doubt about any time-off situation, contact HR.

Share the calendar with your team at the start of each year and encourage advance planning for floating holidays and the Floating Cultural Day.

Eligibility

Regular full-time employees are eligible for paid holiday benefits. Regular part-time employees receive holiday pay only if the holiday falls on a day they are regularly scheduled to work. Employees must be actively employed at the time of the holiday.

Holiday Pay

Holiday pay is based on the hours the employee was scheduled to work on the observed holiday, up to a maximum of 8 hours. Holiday pay for time not worked does not count toward overtime calculations unless required by law.

Holiday During PTO

If a recognized holiday falls during an employee's approved PTO period, the day is treated as a holiday and is not deducted from the employee's PTO balance. Adjust the ADP request accordingly or contact HR to correct it.

Working on a Holiday

Non-exempt employees who work on a recognized holiday receive 1.5× their regular rate for all hours worked that day, plus an alternate paid day off within 14 calendar days. Coordinate with the employee to schedule the alternate day in ADP. Contact HR with questions.

Floating Holidays
Full-time eligible employees receive up to four floating holidays per calendar year. Floating holidays may be used to observe religious or cultural holidays, birthdays, or other days of personal significance, subject to manager approval and business needs.

Key Rules

Requests must be submitted and approved in advance through ADP. Floating holidays must be used within the calendar year in which they are granted. They do not carry over to the following year and are not paid out at termination, unless required by applicable law.

Manager FAQ
Answers to questions managers ask most frequently about PTO, Sick Leave, Paid Holidays, Bereavement, Weekend Flex Days, and managing time off in ADP.
PTO Questions
  • How do I check an employee’s PTO and Sick Leave balance in ADP? ▶
    Log into ADP WorkforceNow. Navigate to My Team → Time Off Balances. You will see each team member’s PTO tier, current PTO balance, and Sick Leave balance. You can also view usage history for the year.
  • How do I tell which PTO tier an employee is assigned to? ▶
    Open the employee’s ADP profile and look for the PTO tier designation (B, C, D, or E) in their work information. The tier is also visible in the Time Off Balances section. If you need details about what their tier allotment is, direct the employee to HR or review their individual policy document provided at hire.
  • An employee has a very high PTO balance. Should I require them to take time off before year-end? ▶
    No — do not mandate time off, but do encourage planning. Remind employees that main company employees can carry over a maximum of 40 hours. Any balance above 40 hours will be forfeited on Dec 31. CA and MA employees have higher carryover caps due to state law. Encourage them to plan in advance if their balance is high. Do not deny reasonable vacation requests.
Sick Leave Questions
  • Can I ask for a doctor’s note when an employee uses sick leave? ▶
    Not for routine absences. A doctor’s note may only be requested after an employee has been absent for three or more consecutive workdays in connection with a single illness or condition. Even then, you must coordinate with HR to ensure the request complies with applicable state law and company policy.
  • Can I ask an employee why they are using sick leave? ▶
    No — do not ask for a diagnosis or reasons. You can confirm that the absence is for a covered purpose (the employee will indicate this in ADP), but you may not ask for details about the nature of the illness, medical condition, or appointment. If an employee feels their manager is asking for medical details in connection with a routine sick leave request, that employee should contact HR.
  • What if a California employee requests sick leave and I am unsure whether it qualifies? ▶
    California sick leave law is broad — it can be used for the employee’s own illness or care needs, family member care, domestic violence situations, and other health-related purposes. If you have any doubt, approve the request and contact HR. CA law is employee-favorable, and the burden is on the employer to prove a request does not qualify.
  • A protected-state employee used all their sick leave mid-year. Can I require them to use PTO for remaining absences? ▶
    No — you may not require or compel an employee to use PTO in lieu of sick leave. Sick leave is a separate, protected benefit. Employees may choose to use PTO to cover an absence after sick leave is exhausted, but the election must come from them. Never force or pressure an employee to switch to PTO. Contact HR with questions.
  • An employee asked to use a floating holiday or the Floating Cultural Day when their sick leave runs out or they don’t have enough sick time — can I approve this? ▶
    No. Floating holidays and the Floating Cultural Day may not be used for wage replacement when sick leave is exhausted or when the sick leave balance is insufficient to cover an absence. These benefits are for scheduled personal, cultural, or religious observances — they are not a substitute for sick leave or PTO in a qualified sick absence scenario. If an employee’s sick leave runs out or is insufficient, direct them to log in to ADP Self Service and submit their time off using the reason code PTO — Call Out if they want wage replacement. Do not approve floating time in this context. Contact HR if you have questions.
Paid Holiday Questions
  • An employee worked a recognized company holiday and is requesting their alternate day off — how do I handle this? ▶
    Non-exempt employees who work on a recognized holiday are entitled to an alternate paid day off in addition to their 1.5x premium pay for hours worked. The alternate day should generally be scheduled within 14 calendar days of the observed holiday. Work with the employee to identify a day that meets both their needs and business requirements. Coordinate the scheduling in ADP. If you have questions about how to process the alternate day in ADP, contact HR.
  • How do I approve a floating holiday request in ADP? ▶
    Employees submit floating holiday requests through ADP Self Service. You will receive a notification to approve or deny the request in ADP WorkforceNow under My Team → Time Off Requests. Review the request for business needs and approve or deny accordingly. Floating holidays must be submitted and approved in advance — they may not be retroactively applied to an unplanned absence or used in place of sick leave.
Weekend Flex Day Questions
  • An exempt employee traveled for business on a weekend and is requesting a flex day — what do I do? ▶
    Review the request to confirm the employee is exempt and that the travel was required business travel away from their regular work location (not just remote work, emails, or calls). If it qualifies, approve a flex day to be taken within 14 calendar days. Flex days do not reduce PTO balances and require your explicit approval — they are not automatic. If operational needs prevent use within 14 days, you may approve an alternate timeframe and document it. Contact HR if you are unsure whether a specific situation qualifies.
  • Can I deny a Weekend Travel Flex Day request? ▶
    Yes. Flex days are discretionary and subject to your approval based on business needs. You are not required to approve every request. You may also reschedule a flex day if the originally requested date creates a business conflict. However, do not deny a request solely because the employee is exempt — the policy applies to eligible exempt employees. If travel was required and the employee is eligible, a denial should be based on a legitimate business reason. When in doubt, contact HR.
Bereavement Questions
  • How do I process a bereavement leave request in ADP? ▶
    The employee submits the request directly in ADP WorkforceNow by selecting leave type “Bereavement Leave.” You approve it in ADP. Bereavement leave does not reduce the employee’s PTO or Sick Leave balance — do not recode it as PTO or Sick Leave. If the employee needs more time than the policy provides, they may submit a separate PTO request. Contact HR if you have questions about documentation or if the employee works in California or Illinois.
  • A California employee is taking bereavement leave — what additional rules apply? ▶
    California employees with 30+ days of tenure are entitled to 5 bereavement days (vs. 3 under the main policy). Days 1–3 are paid at base rate. Days 4–5 are unpaid — the employee may elect to use PTO or sick leave to cover those days, but you must not apply either bank automatically. The employee’s written request is required. CA bereavement also covers a broader family definition (including parent-in-law and any person with an “equivalent of family relationship”) and must be completed within 3 months of the date of death. Documentation (death certificate, obituary, or written verification) is required within 30 days. Contact HR or legal leadership before processing any CA bereavement request involving Days 4–5.
  • An Illinois employee notified me of a qualifying event under the Family Bereavement Leave Act (FBLA) — what do I do? ▶
    Contact HR immediately. The Illinois FBLA applies to FMLA-eligible employees and covers qualifying events including the death of a family member and reproductive loss events (miscarriage, failed IVF, stillbirth, failed adoption, diagnosis affecting fertility). Eligible employees may take up to 10 workdays per qualifying event and up to 6 weeks in a 12-month rolling period. Leave is unpaid, but employees may elect to use available PTO — do not apply PTO automatically. The leave must be logged as FBLA in ADP. Reproductive loss events must be handled with strict confidentiality — do not discuss details with your team or other managers. No adverse action may be taken before, during, or after this leave.
General Questions
  • One of my NYC employees is taking protected time off and also asked to use PTO for wage replacement. How do I handle this? ▶
    The employee’s request is valid. Direct them to submit the PTO request to HR directly — do not apply PTO yourself. HR will process it correctly in ADP. Remember: the Protected Time Off bank is still reduced based on the time away, even when the employee uses PTO for pay. Job protection remains in place regardless. The process is: sick leave first (if applicable), then protected time off (unpaid), then PTO if elected by the employee.
  • An employee asked me something about their leave that I am not sure how to answer — what should I do? ▶
    Submit an HR Request using the HR Request Form. Leave policies — especially state-specific sick leave, FMLA, and bereavement rules — have legal nuances that go beyond a manager’s day-to-day scope. When in doubt, it is always better to check with HR before giving an employee guidance that turns out to be incorrect.
Weekend Travel Flex Day — Exempt Employees
A limited flexibility benefit for exempt salaried employees required to travel for business on a weekend. This is not overtime, comp time, or additional wages.
Manager reminder: This policy applies only to exempt employees. Do not approve flex days for non-exempt employees — doing so could create overtime liability. Flex days are discretionary, subject to your approval, and must generally be used within 14 calendar days.
Policy Overview
Eligibility
Who It Applies To
Exempt salaried employees required to travel for Company business on a Saturday or Sunday, involving travel away from their regular work location. Weekend work without required travel does not qualify.
Qualifying
What Counts as Travel
Required business travel on a Sat or Sun away from the employee's regular work location. Incidental remote work, weekend emails, calls, or minimal work without required travel does not qualify for a flex day.
Flex Day
One Day, Your Approval
An eligible employee may request one flex day following qualifying weekend travel. It must generally be taken within 14 calendar days. You may approve, deny, or reschedule it based on business needs. Flex days do not reduce PTO balances.
What Flex Days Are — And Are Not
✓ Flex Days ARE:
  • A discretionary flexibility benefit
  • Subject to your approval and business needs
  • To be used within 14 calendar days (extendable by you for operational needs)
  • Separate from PTO — do not reduce the employee's PTO balance
  • For exempt employees only
✗ Flex Days ARE NOT:
  • Overtime compensation or comp time
  • An entitlement — they require your approval
  • Earned for weekend remote work without travel
  • Accrued, vested, or carried over
  • Paid out at separation
  • Applicable to non-exempt employees
Your Role as a Manager
  1. When an exempt employee travels on a weekend for Company business, they may request a flex day from you.
  2. Review the request — confirm travel was required and away from their regular work location.
  3. Approve, deny, or reschedule based on business needs. You are not required to approve every request.
  4. If approved, coordinate the flex day schedule. It should generally be within 14 calendar days.
  5. If operational needs prevent use within 14 days, you may approve an alternate timeframe — document this.
  6. You may require reasonable documentation confirming the qualifying weekend travel.
  7. If you have questions about whether a specific situation qualifies, contact HR before approving.
Flex days not used within 14 calendar days of the qualifying weekend travel will expire unless you approve an alternative timeframe due to operational needs. Flex days do not carry over and do not accumulate. This policy may be modified, suspended, or discontinued by the Company at any time.
